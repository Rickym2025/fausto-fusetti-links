The Sentimental Value: Joachim Trier's Film Review and the Importance of Homes

The Sentimental Value, Joachim Trier's latest film, has arrived in Italian cinemas with an impressive nine Oscar nominations. More than just a movie, it's a profound reflection on family, cinema itself as a narrative tool, the pain that inevitably marks our lives, and, above all, the intrinsic value of the places we inhabit and that, in a way, contribute to defining who we are.

An Analysis of the Central Themes

The film stands out for its ability to intertwine various complex themes. Family, with its often intricate and painful dynamics, is at the heart of the narrative. Trier doesn't sugarcoat reality but presents an authentic and sometimes ruthless snapshot of family ties, misunderstandings, and silences that can erode even the deepest affections.

Another crucial theme is cinema itself. The Sentimental Value is also a meta-textual reflection on the power of cinema to immortalize moments, emotions, and people, and on its role in shaping our perception of reality. Trier plays with narrative conventions, offering the viewer different points of view and leaving room for personal interpretation.

Pain, finally, is a constant that permeates the entire work. A dull, sometimes unexpressed pain, which manifests itself through gestures, glances, and unspoken words. A pain that unites the characters and pushes them to seek meaning in their existence.

The Importance of Places: Homes as a Mirror of the Soul

One of the most original and fascinating aspects of the film is the centrality of places, particularly homes, as real characters. Homes are not just scenarios but spaces full of meaning, custodians of memories, silent witnesses of human events. Each house tells a story, reflects the personality of those who inhabit it, and contributes to defining their identity.

The houses in The Sentimental Value are far from perfect. They are lived-in houses, with the marks of time, imperfections, and traces of those who have lived in them. They are houses that speak of joys and sorrows, of loves and losses, of dreams and disappointments. They are houses that, ultimately, remind us that life is a journey made of ups and downs, of happy moments and difficult times.

Style and Technique: Joachim Trier's Direction

Joachim Trier's direction is elegant and refined, but at the same time austere and essential. The Norwegian director doesn't indulge in unnecessary virtuosity but puts his technique at the service of the narrative, creating an intimate and engaging atmosphere. The photography is meticulous, with a color palette that helps create a melancholic and reflective atmosphere.

The film's pace is slow and measured, in line with its contemplative nature. Trier takes the time to delve into the characters, explore their emotions, and reveal their secrets. The soundtrack is evocative and appropriate, capable of amplifying the emotional effect of the images.

An Intense and Moving Work

The Sentimental Value is an intense and moving film, which invites the viewer to reflect on their own life, their affections, and the meaning of the places they inhabit. A work that will not fail to arouse strong emotions and leave an indelible mark in the hearts of those who watch it. The director's ability to treat universal themes with sensitivity and depth, combined with impeccable direction and a cast of exceptional actors, make The Sentimental Value a film not to be missed.
