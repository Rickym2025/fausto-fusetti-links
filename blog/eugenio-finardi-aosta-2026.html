Eugenio Finardi Sets Aosta Ablaze in 2026: Review of the Definitive Live Bootleg

Straight from the Teatro Splendor in Aosta, a dive into the past and future of Eugenio Finardi's music. This bootleg, number 365 in the series, immortalizes the performance of January 16, 2026, an event that celebrated the 50-year career of the Milanese artist. A concert that, as the original article points out, was a true "first time on the web."

A Sonic Journey Through Classics and Novelties

The evening in Aosta, part of the "Tutto '75-'25 Tour," retraced the fundamental stages of Eugenio Finardi's discography, starting from his historic debut album, Non gettate alcun oggetto dal finestrino (Don't Throw Anything Out the Window). But it wasn't just about nostalgia. The concert, as highlighted in Chris's review published on AostaSera, managed to combine the solidity of roots with a surprising ability to look ahead.

The Alchemy with the Band

A key element of the success of this live performance is the understanding between Eugenio Finardi and his band, composed of talented musicians such as Giovanni 'Giuvazza' Maggiore on guitar, Maximilian Agostini on keyboards, and Claudio Arfinengo on drums. In particular, the collaboration with Giovanni Maggiore has been defined as a true "complicity," with a significant impact also on the composition of the most recent tracks.

A Tribute to Ivan Graziani

During the concert, Finardi wanted to pay tribute to another great of Italian music, Ivan Graziani, interpreting his touching Il prete di Anghiari. A particularly intense moment, which demonstrated Finardi's deep admiration for the singer-songwriter from Abruzzo.

Timeless Classics and Important Messages

Naturally, the great classics were not missing, such as La forza dell'amore and Le ragazze di Osaka, which made the Splendor's audience sing and dance. And then, songs like La Radio, with its unambiguous message in favor of free information, which demonstrate how Eugenio Finardi's music is still profoundly relevant today and capable of making people think.

An Engaging and Promising Finale

The concert concluded with a choral performance of Extraterrestre, a symbolic song that united generations of fans. And, as reported by AostaSera, even after the end of the show, Eugenio Finardi proved available and open to dialogue with his audience, promising to continue writing and exciting with his music.
