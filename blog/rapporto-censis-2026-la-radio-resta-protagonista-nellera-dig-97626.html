La radio nell'era digitale

Censis 2026 Report: Radio Remains a Key Player in the Digital Age

The Censis 2026 Report has recently been published, providing a comprehensive overview of the Italian media landscape. Among the most interesting data, radio emerges as a central player, continuing to be a fundamental means of communication for Italians.

Radio in the Digital Age

According to the report's data, radio remains a very popular means of communication, with over 70% of Italians listening to the radio at least once a week. This figure is particularly interesting, considering the exponential growth of digital media in recent years.

Lorenzo Suraci commented on the report's data, highlighting the importance of radio in the digital age. According to Suraci, radio offers a unique and personal experience that cannot be replicated by digital media.

The Key Points of the Report

  • Radio remains a very popular means of communication, with over 70% of Italians listening to the radio at least once a week.
  • Young Italians are the biggest users of radio, with 60% of them listening to the radio every day.
  • Radio offers a unique and personal experience that cannot be replicated by digital media.

In conclusion, the Censis 2026 Report confirms radio's central role in the Italian media landscape. Radio remains a fundamental means of communication, offering a unique and personal experience to listeners.
