Why Celebrate World Radio Day?

World Radio Day, proclaimed by UNESCO in 2011 and celebrated every year on February 13th (although celebrations and related events often extend for several days, as in this case), aims to raise public awareness about the importance of radio as a mass communication tool. Despite the advent of new technologies and digital platforms, radio continues to be a fundamental means for:

  • Disseminating information: Radio reaches even the most remote and isolated areas, providing news and updates in real-time.
  • Promoting education: Many radio stations offer educational and cultural programs.
  • Stimulating debate: Radio fosters the exchange of ideas and opinions on current affairs.
  • Entertaining: Radio offers a wide range of entertainment programs, from music to comedy shows.

An Event for Everyone

World Radio Day 2026 in Milan is not only an opportunity to meet your favorite speakers but also a chance to participate in workshops, conferences, and debates on the future of radio. The event is aimed at:

  • Industry professionals: Speakers, journalists, technicians, producers.
  • Communication students: A chance to learn and network in the world of radio.
  • Radio enthusiasts: All those who love listening to the radio and want to learn more about how it works.

The Future of Radio

World Radio Day is an opportunity to reflect on the role that radio will continue to play in the future. In an increasingly digital and connected world, radio is reinventing itself, leveraging new technologies to reach an ever-wider and more diverse audience. The radio of the future will be increasingly interactive, personalized, and accessible from any device. But one thing is certain: its voice will continue to resonate in our homes, in our cars, and in our lives.
